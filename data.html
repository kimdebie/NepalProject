The data

The graphs you see here and on the home page display data from two sources: conventional data, collected in a top-down manner by aid organizations like the United Nations (see here for more information). This data is made public in the form of ‘situational reports’, which are published daily or every two days and contain everything that is known about the situation on the ground. The crowdsourced data that this project builds on comes from Humanity Road. This NGO also publishes situational reports, but bases them on crowdsourced data.

Kim de Bie (11077379), University of Amsterdam, January 2016
